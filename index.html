About

I am a fourth-year PhD student at New York University specializing in music cognition, machine learning, and digital signal processing. My work explores how music impacts the brain, combining cognitive science with machine learning to create innovative music technology applications. I have developed projects like SoundSignature, an app that integrates music information retrieval and AI to provide personalized insights into users' favorite songs. I’m also experienced in computational models of music perception, audio feature extraction, and real-time music generation.

  • Programming: Advanced: Python, Matlab, R | Intermediate: C++, JavaScript, HTML
  • Research: Machine Learning, Computational Modeling, Inferential Statistics
  • Audio and Music: Librosa, essentia, MIRToolbox, madmom, mirdata, mir_eval, music21, mido
  • Frameworks: PyTorch, TensorFlow, Keras, Node.js
  • Tools & Technologies: Git, Logic Pro X, JIRA

My current focus is on leveraging technology to deepen our understanding of music’s role in cognitive processes and creating applications that make music-related data accessible and insightful.
